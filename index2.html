Why does wine taste different than grape juice?

Sluurrrp I can imagine that we've all had grape juice. That delicious and tangy fruit that is so refreshing on a warm summer day. I also imagine that you've wondered how that delicious, sweet fruit can turn into a bevrage that make your parents go wild. Let me tell you, it's fermentation. Fermentation is an extremely complex process but let's boil it down. Simply put, when a food/drink sits out in the heat it gets infected with yeast, mold, & bacteria. This is often a bad thing. Mold can make your PB&J look all nasty and disgusting. However, when you put something in a carefully made environment, like wine, it becomes fermented in a good way. It produces alchohol (the crazy stuff we mentioned before) and the grape juice becomes extremely tart.

Fer•men•ta•tion
noun
A process where yeasts, bacteria, and other living organisms come and eat another food.
